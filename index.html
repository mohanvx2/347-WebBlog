Blog Entry #1:

Sep 10th, 2020

Readers,

These past two weeks in CS 347 we primarily focused on getting introduced into HTML and CSS. Through a mix of lectures and synchronous lab meetings I've learned a good deal of how websites are constructed at a basic level. One particularly helpful resource was Chapter 4 of Client-side Web Development. It gave good examples and helpful information on the general structure of websites including footers, headings and more. In addition our synchronous labs were a great way to bounce ideas off each other and helped to improve our collaboration skills.

Another useful technique we learned was the use of flex boxes. This allows us to re-order and manipulate the content of webpages on a larger scale. Prior to this different padding/positional values and tricks were the only methods I knew of how to change up the structure of a page. Hopefully I can make some use of these techniques to improve this page in the future!

Thank you all for reading!

- Varun
